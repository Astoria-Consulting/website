CORE BUSINESS TECHNOLOGY SOLUTION

Project Overview

The Craft Guys Trading Company needs a technology solution that will allow them to sell craft beer and other alcoholic beverages to businesses and directly to consumers.

This will be achieved with a visually minimal ecommerce website, product information management, customer data management, inventory, invoicing, order management, subscriptions, a blog, contact management, supplier relation management with activity tracking, and a wholesale storefront.

They need these solutions in one user friendly system, using modern technology, with the data stored in a way that can be analyzed. This data will be a key driver of business decisions through relevant dashboards and performance metrics, which are generated automatically.

Solution

The entire software solution will be a customized instance of Spree Ecommerce and will be hosted on Heroku.

Spree is an open source platform for ecommerce that is trusted by many startups up to companies as large as GoDaddy. This solution allows the Craft Guys to store their data in databases, giving them all the benefits of modern technology from speed to analysis. Hosting in the cloud allows incredibly high uptime and automated scaling, so the solution can easily accomodate this fast growing business.

Adding and editing product data (attributes, images, prices, etc.), is all done through an administration interface. Craft Guys is responsible for maintaining and updating this product data, as it's used to feed the customer facing website.

Implementation

An MVP (Minimum Viable Product) and Agile approach will be followed. The MVP will be developed and deployed first, but that doesn't mean the site is live to the public. It means the Craft Guys have access to use the system, give critiques and requests (which are included as part of the initial build), and watch the progress as features are added over time. The site can go live at any point during development, after the MVP is complete. The go-live will not affect development, as the agreed upon features will continue to deploy after the go-live.
